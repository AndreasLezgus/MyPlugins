### 1 — STRUCTURE (Substack Growth Format)

Use this proven Substack structure that drives reads, shares, and conversions:

**a) TITLE (max 8 words)**

- Pattern: [Counterintuitive claim] or [Specific number + outcome]
- Must create a curiosity gap — the reader needs to click to resolve it
- No clickbait. The article must deliver on the title’s promise.
- Examples of good patterns: “Why Your AI Strategy Is Already Obsolete” / “The 3 Questions Every CTO Ignores About AI Security”

**b) SUBTITLE (1 sentence)**

- Expands on the title. Adds specificity or stakes.
- Pattern: “What [audience] gets wrong about [topic] — and what to do instead.”

**c) HOOK (first 2–3 sentences, visible in preview)**

- Start with a bold, specific claim, a surprising data point, or a short real-world scenario.
- No throat-clearing (“In today’s fast-paced world…”). Jump straight in.
- This is the most important part — it determines whether email subscribers open or scroll past.

**d) BODY (800–1,200 words)**

- Use short paragraphs (2–4 sentences max).
- Use subheadings that are **mini-arguments**, not labels. (“The real cost isn’t money — it’s trust” \> “Cost Analysis”)
- Weave in 1–2 brief, concrete examples from public sector / enterprise / security context.
- Include 2–3 rhetorical questions as “door openers” to keep the reader engaged.
- Bold key phrases sparingly — only for anchor concepts the reader should remember.
- Write for scanners AND deep readers: someone skimming subheadings and bold text should still get the core argument.

**e) QUICK-CHECK LIST (at the end of the article)**

- Introduce with a short transition like: *“Before you close this tab — here’s your practical takeaway.”*
- Provide 5–7 actionable checklist items the reader can apply **this week**.
- Format: ✅ [Action verb] + [specific action] + [why it matters in one clause]
- These must be genuinely useful, not generic filler.

**f) CLOSING + CTA (Subscribe nudge)**

- End with a brief, warm call-to-action. No hard sell.
- Pattern: Tie back to the article’s core message, then invite the reader to subscribe.
- Use this style:

> *If this gave you one idea worth testing — consider subscribing. I publish weekly with practical frameworks for AI leaders who need clarity, not hype.*
> 
> **[Subscribe](https://andreaslezgus.substack.com)**

### 2 — TONE & STYLE RULES

| Rule                | Details                                                                                                                                                                          |
| ------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Sentence length** | Short. 10–20 words. No nested subordinate clauses.                                                                                                                               |
| **Jargon**          | Use technical terms only when they add precision. Always explain briefly on first use.                                                                                           |
| **Authority**       | Establish through specifics (numbers, project types, real constraints) — never through self-promotion.                                                                           |
| **Humor**           | Dry, understated, especially at the end. Never sarcastic toward the reader.                                                                                                      |
| **Perspective**     | Write as “experienced peer” — not lecturer, not guru.                                                                                                                            |
| **Criticism**       | Always constructive: Problem → Analysis → Path forward. Never name-and-shame individuals.                                                                                        |
| **“We” framing**    | Use “we” when transitioning to solutions — it signals shared responsibility.                                                                                                     |
| **Avoid**           | Buzzwords without substance (“game-changer”, “disruptive”, “paradigm shift”), listicle formats in the body, emojis (except in the Quick-Check list), excessive exclamation marks |

### 3 — SEO & DISCOVERABILITY (Substack-specific)
